About Suriname

 

The country of Suriname is a northeastern country in South America. Being a relatively small and former Dutch plantation colony it is home to a diverse ethnic populace and a very interesting and rich culture. It gained independence on November 25th 1975, and has since been going through multiple regime changes and is currently under civilian rule in a democratic-assembly independent republic or (DAIR). 

The country is a neighbor to French Guiana and is thus affected by French culture, as well as a spurt of other influences from Indian, Muslim, Indonesian and African cultures. Thus the list of cultural influences is extremely diverse. Most of the population are descendants of laborers from India whom consist of 25% of the population the next largest demographic would be the Maroons who are descended of African slaves whom had escaped the slave trade at about 20% of the population. Creoles (subset of Africans) make up about 10-20%, and the last big group of Surnamese people are the Javanese Indonesian people from Java making up about 7-14% of the population. 

The remaining portion of the population is not particularly well documented but is likely a mix of South American natives and European settlers of mixed proportions. Despite the Dutch having a massive influence on culture and language and Dutch being the national language it is unlikely to be a Surinamese’ person’s first language with it being taught as a secondary language in most parts of the country.
